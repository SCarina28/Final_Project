1956

The existence of our school is but undeniable example of what necessity means. Owing it's existence, the area presently occupied by our school and Dr. Jose P. Rizal Elementary School as it has been excluded from the operation of Resolution no. 20 series 1910 of the Philippine Commission reserving the area as Public Park and recreational ground.

Accounting to the historicl establishment and operation of our school merely bring us to the fact of growing student population which obviously needed to be addressed as mandated for by the constitution that decent education should be freely provided to our youth.

1968

Through the leadership of Vice-mayor Sinforoso Fangonil who passed a resolution numbered 183 acting on the clamor of the Federation of Parents and Teachers association for the opening of annex high schools in such areas in the city. The first operation of the school Baguio City National High School-Rizal Annex has two prefab rooms of then Pacdal Elementary School now Jose P. Rizal Elementary School were made for them to occupy as their classrooms; each classrooms consists of thirty freshmen students.

Through the years, improvements were evidently observed. There were significant increase in the number of teachers as classrooms and bildings multiplied catering enrollees gaining in number.

2013

The nationalization of Baguio City National High School-Rizal Annex into Rizal National high school among other annexes of Baguio City National High School came possible after Republic Act 10479 authored by then Congressman Engr. Bernardo V. Vergara and was signed into law by his Excellency President Benigno Simeon C. Aquino III.

Present

Today, as Rizal National High School gains its age, it continuously withstands the role of molding young men and women with its current dynamic administrator and passionate preserving teachers who continuously provide quality learning services developing them to be virtually assets of their own communities and our nationthe Republic of the Philippines.
